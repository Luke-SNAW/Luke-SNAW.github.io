Distinguish that from which

That and which both introduce subordinate clauses. What's the difference between them? Well, in some countries, the two words are pretty much interchangeable. Inevitably though, alert readers from the United States will angrily announce that you confused the two words again.

In the United States, reserve which for nonessential subordinate clauses, and use that for an essential subordinate clause that the sentence can't live without. For example, the key message in the following sentence is that Python is an interpreted language; the sentence can survive without Guido van Rossum invented:

Python is an interpreted language, which Guido van Rossum invented.

By contrast, the following sentence requires don't involve linear algebra:

Fortran is perfect for mathematical calculations that don't involve linear algebra.

If you read a sentence aloud and hear a pause just before the subordinate clause, then use which. If you don't hear a pause, use that. Go back and read the preceding two example sentences. Do you hear the pause in the first sentence?

Place a comma before which; do not place a comma before that.

How to Use Em Dashes (–), En Dashes (–), and Hyphens (-)

  • Hyphens connect things, such as compound words: double-decker, cut-and-dried, 212-555-5555.
  • EN dashes make a range between things: Boston–San Francisco flight, 10–20 years: both connect not only the endpoints, but define that all the space between is included. (Compare the last usage with the phone number example under Hyphens.)
  • EM dashes break things, such as sentences or thoughts: 'What the—!'; A paragraph should express one idea—but rules are made to be broken. — mmooss

"as"의 용법과 뉘앙스

Trump heads to China for trade talks as Iran war continues

이 문장에서 as는 "~하는 동안 / ~하는 시점에" 라는 시간적 동시성을 나타냅니다.

이 문장의 구조

Trump heads to China for trade talks as Iran war continues

→ 트럼프가 무역 협상을 위해 중국으로 향하는 가운데, 이란 전쟁은 계속되고 있다

두 가지 사건이 동시에 진행 중임을 강조합니다.

"as"의 주요 용법 5가지

용법의미예시
시간 (동시성)~하는 동안, ~하면서He smiled as she walked in.
이유/원인~때문에, ~이므로As it was raining, we stayed in.
양보~임에도 불구하고Try as he might, he couldn't win.
비교/방식~처럼, ~대로Do as I say.
자격/역할~로서He works as a doctor.

헤드라인에서 "as"의 특별한 뉘앙스

뉴스 헤드라인에서 as는 단순한 "동시성" 이상의 뉘앙스를 담습니다.

① 대조적 병치 (Contrastive Juxtaposition) 두 사건을 나란히 놓아 아이러니나 긴장감을 만들어냅니다.

  • 외교(무역 협상) ↔ 전쟁(이란) — 대비되는 두 상황을 동시에 제시

② 인과 암시 명시적으로 "because"라고 하지 않지만, 독자가 관계를 추론하도록 유도합니다.

  • "전쟁이 계속되는 상황이기 때문에, 이 협상이 더 의미 있다"는 맥락 암시

③ 압축성 헤드라인은 짧아야 하므로 as 하나로 while / because / even though를 동시에 함축합니다.

같은 문장, 다른 접속사로 바꾸면?

접속사뉘앙스 변화
while순수한 시간적 동시성, 중립적
as동시성 + 대조/긴장감, 약한 인과 암시
even as더 강한 대조 ("~에도 불구하고 동시에")
because명확한 인과관계

Trump heads to China even as Iran war continues. → "전쟁이 한창인데도 불구하고" — 훨씬 강한 아이러니 강조
